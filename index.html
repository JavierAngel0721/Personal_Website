About me

Hello! I'm a senior Computer Engineering student at Virginia Tech, graduating in December with a concentration in Networking and Cybersecurity, but my true passion lies in embedded systems, firmware development, and hardware-software integration. Over the course of my academic and professional journey, I've developed deep hands-on experience building, programming, and debugging embedded platforms, from low-level microcontroller programming to full system integration. My coursework and projects have given me a strong foundation in computer architecture, digital design, and hardware-level problem solving, which I’ve applied in both academic settings and real-world defense-related work with NAVSEA. I’m currently working with Southwest Research Institute (SwRI) on the development of a custom PCB that will interface with a CCD sensor onboard a satellite for deep space imaging. My primary role focuses on writing the firmware for the board, ensuring it adheres to the Interface Control Document (ICD) and meets all system-level requirements provided by SwRI. This involves close collaboration with hardware engineers and precise low-level control to ensure reliable operation in a space-grade environment.

My core strengths lie in firmware engineering, baremetal programming, and hardware description languages. I’m proficient in C, C++, Python, Verilog, and HDL, and have worked extensively with Arduino and Raspberry Pi platforms. I’ve implemented communication protocols like SPI and UART, performed signal analysis, and written low-level firmware to interface with peripherals. My hardware skills extend to FPGA development, RTL design, and transistor-level understanding, along with strong debugging skills using tools like CMake, pytest, multimeters, signal analyzers, and GPIO interfaces. I also have experience with microcontroller architecture, particularly MIPS 32-bit, and have worked through design and analysis at the assembly level. During my time with NAVSEA, I contributed to application integration, hardware/software validation, and signal analysis within complex embedded and distributed systems.

In addition to embedded systems work, I bring full-stack technical versatility. I'm experienced in C#, HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and scripting with Ansible. I've developed multithreaded applications, managed Docker containers, implemented server-client communication, and deployed systems using Proxmox and Kubernetes. I also enjoy building and maintaining my own hardware—whether it’s PC building and diagnostics, maintaining a self-hosted Proxmox server, or designing and fabricating parts with SolidWorks and 3D printing technologies using CAD and slicer tools. While I have a strong grasp of networking protocols such as TCP/IP and UDP, and experience with cybersecurity principles, my primary focus and interest are in embedded systems, low-level programming, and the seamless integration of hardware and software into efficient, reliable systems.
